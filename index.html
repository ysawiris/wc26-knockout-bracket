    How It Works

    The pool, in four steps

    1. 1
      Set the order. The 12 teams snake-draft in last-season finish order. Flip best-first / worst-first on the Draft Order tab.
    2. 2
      Draft your two. Snake draft means each team grabs one stronger and one weaker of the 32 Round-of-32 countries — 24 picks total.
    3. 3
      Advance to score. Every round your country wins is worth points — R32 3, R16 5, QF 8, SF 13, Final 21 — plus 0.1 per goal.
    4. 4
      Most points wins. Ties break on advancement points, then draft slot. Cards and fouls are shown but never count.

    Scoring, round by round

    Every time one of your countries wins a round, you bank that round's points. The values follow the Fibonacci sequence (3, 5, 8, 13, 21) — so surviving deep is worth far more than a pile of early wins.

    Round your team winsThis winRunning total
    Round of 32+33
    Round of 16+58
    Quarterfinal+816
    Semifinal → reaches Final+1329
    Final — Champion 👑+2150

    The running total is your team's score if that's as far as it gets: out in the R32 = 0, a runner-up that loses the final = 29, a champion = 50. No single team can outscore a champion.

    Goals add up too

    Every goal your country scores = +0.1 points. Goals count in every match they play — even one they lose — so a high-scoring team pays off even on a short run.

    Example: your team scores 3 goals in the Round of 32, then gets knocked out. You bank +0.3 from the goals on top of the 0 advancement — and a team that reaches the quarterfinal scoring 5 along the way earns 3 + 5 + 8 + 0.5 = 16.5.

    Yellow cards, red cards and fouls are tracked on the site for color, but they never score.

    Do you need the champion to win?

    Owning the eventual World Cup winner (worth 50) guarantees you first place — no other pair of teams can mathematically catch a champion.

    But remember: 8 of the 32 countries go undrafted. If a free-pool underdog shocks the world and lifts the trophy, the title is wide open — and whoever holds the runner-up (29) plus a deep semifinalist (16) takes the league. So a finalist usually wins it, but the runner-up and deep teams keep everyone alive to the final whistle.

    World Cup 2026 — the knockout

    The biggest World Cup ever: 48 teams, three hosts (United States, Mexico, Canada), 104 matches. After the group stage, 32 teams reach the brand-new Round of 32 — and that's where our pool lives.

    The bracket is single-elimination: R32 → R16 → quarterfinals → semifinals → final, 31 matches in all. Top seeds can only meet in the final (1v32, 2v31, …). The final is July 19 at MetLife Stadium, New York / New Jersey.

    For the pool, only the knockout counts. You own two countries; you score every round they survive plus a small bonus for the goals they score. The further they go, the more you win.
